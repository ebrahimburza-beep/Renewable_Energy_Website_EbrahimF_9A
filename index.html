Home

This website explores the causes and effects of global warming, possible solutions through renewable energy, and the actions each of us can take to make a difference.

Problem

The main cause of global warming is the excessive emission of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere and raise the Earth's average temperature. The result is climate change, rising sea levels, droughts, and the loss of biodiversity.

Burning coal, oil, and gas for energy releases massive amounts of carbon dioxide. Power plants, vehicles, and industries are the biggest contributors. This directly increases atmospheric CO₂ and traps more heat.

Trees absorb carbon dioxide, but large-scale deforestation reduces the planet's ability to remove CO₂ from the air. Forest loss for urbanization and agriculture worsens climate imbalance.

Factories release pollutants and gases during production. Industrial waste and manufacturing also add methane and nitrous oxide, further damaging the atmosphere.

Solution

Renewable energy offers clean, reliable, and sustainable power. Unlike fossil fuels, renewables do not emit harmful gases and are constantly replenished by natural processes. Shifting to renewable energy is one of the most effective ways to slow global warming and protect the environment for future generations.

  • Solar Energy: Converts sunlight into electricity using solar panels. It is affordable, long-lasting, and ideal for homes and businesses.
  • Wind Energy: Wind turbines generate power without releasing emissions. They are especially effective in coastal and open regions.
  • Hydro Power: Uses flowing water to produce electricity. It is renewable but must be managed carefully to avoid affecting ecosystems.

Renewable sources combined with efficient technology can lead us to a clean, balanced, and sustainable world.

Possible Action

  1. Reduce energy use at home by turning off unused lights, using efficient appliances, and supporting green power programs.
  2. Plant trees and support afforestation drives to balance the carbon cycle.
  3. Spread awareness among peers and communities about the benefits of renewable energy.
  4. Support policies and companies that prioritize sustainable energy solutions.

Even small individual steps can create large collective change when done consistently.
